John Doe

As a local resident who will be directly impacted by this development, I feel compelled to express my concerns regarding this proposed development.

I accept that there is a requirement and fundamental need to decarbonise the UK economy, there are other ways to achieve this which are less environmentally damaging. By investing in initiatives such as rooftop solar both for homes and industrial buildings, we can achieve the same without removing loads of land across the UK from the agricultural system. The idea for this solarfarm is a prime example of this.

The building of a huge solarfarm around these small towns is inappropriate for the area, my uncle John would not be happy about it. The removal of significant amounts of productive farmland will only result in increased challenges to providing food for England.

The original proposal was for a normal solarfarm. The scheme now proposes a big solar farm with big batteries. The original proposal was for the battery storage to be located at a disused powerplant. This proposal was much more appropriate as it was further away from my house at 8 Timsbury lane and instead provided a direct link into the National Grid.
